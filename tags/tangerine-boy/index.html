19 MAY 2025

fuck- i remember, i remember that first day Will took me out. I remember thinking I kinda knew some birds but had no idea the vastness of the world of birding. I remember telling him a bird made a certain call that was absolutely wrong and he just nodded and let it go. He was so patient and I could tell, even then, that he knew I had no idea what I was getting myself into. He could sense that there was something in me that would latch on and figure it out. Here I am at the first sip of my inaugural New England Summer. Will is moving on and I’ve been having a hard time with that. I’ve actually been dealing with a lot of rough emotions around him, but I’m starting to have peace with it. I wish so much that I could spend more time with him. I wish I could see him as often as I do for the foreseeable future, but I can’t. We’re at two different places in life. But I’m a birder now. I’m a birder thanks to him, but also thanks to my crazy autistic brain. I get so excited by these creatures marching and fluttering around outside. They’re dumb, they’re sweet, they’re sometimes pretty annoying, but they’re beautiful. It’s lovely to have so many reasons to go outside. I can’t believe I rushed Annie out of my house because I wanted to go to Mansfield Hollow before twilight was over. I should mention that, of course, she was invited, but she had things to take care of. I’m beginning to not just recognize birds by sight, song, call, and name, but people in the birding community too. I’m learning names of people who frequent the eBird reports in Connecticut. I’ve met three other prominent birders around my age and one lovely gentleman who showed Will and I where the West Virginia Whites were. I’ve even made pals with the David Mathieu. Which I have to admit, I had no idea how much of a celebrity he was until after I began birding with him. I’ll have to tell him sometime about my naive self emailing him and Aiden Kiley on the fly asking to bird together. I’m so thankful David replied, I feel we click and I feel we could get to become good pals. I’d like to invite him to a yard party when we have one this summer. Invite him to see some art or other non-birding things. I wonder what his life is like outside of birding. I wondered that about Will for a while, but I slowly learned that, other than school, he spends much of his private time surfing the naturalist web. I’ll be eternally grateful for Will for giving me almost an entire year of teaching. Without him, I would have never gotten to the point I am at such a swift pace. It was around this time last year I was wishing I could identify birds the way I can now. I remember the chattahoochee valley beats like a heart and hearing all the birds. I was the whippoorwill, after all.. I remember being in California wishing I were a birder because I’d be in heaven. It was just a year ago and I can’t believe it’s been just a year. I’m actually a birder now, and I can honestly say I haven’t been so happy in such a long time.
