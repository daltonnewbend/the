Jan. 10, 2026

Common Goldeneye

Napatree Point | US-RI

On top of the dune while scoping mergansers and eiders, I spotted a single goldeneye. I was a bit unsure of myself, but a few moments later over at the lighthouse I saw a dozen of them together on the water.

Jan. 10, 2026

Black Scoter

Napatree Point | US-RI

Posted up on the sand dune that marks your ’entrance’ to Napatree Point, I scoped out hundreds of Red-brested Mergansers. Probably one of the most significant times I’ve seen them. I caught a glimpse of a very black duck with a bright orange bill. I was certain what I was looking at. I found it several times after my first spotting, but was unable to capture a photo. Fortunately over at the lighthouse, I was able to see another and get a photo.

Purple Sandpiper

Correct.

Long-tailed Tit

Orto Botanico di Firenze | IT-52 Royal Botanic Garden | GB-SCT

I encountered this bird during study abroad in Italy. I recorded lots of birdsong for a project on [[tesselates]] I was working on.

I later re-encountered this bird on a trip to the UK to visit my friend, Rachael, where it was heard at Royal Botanic Garden in Edinburgh and several other places. I consider this encounter my first ’life’ encounter.

Jul. 15, 2017

Black-billed Magpie

Chautauqua Park | US-CO

I encountered this bird on the way down from a hike in the Flat Irons outside of Boulder, CO. I had forgotten about this bird until fall of 2024 when I went through my media of birds. Before becoming a birder, I would often document birds with the intention of figuring out what they were at a later date. Seven years later, I rediscovered the video and the memory crept back into consciousness.

Nov. 14, 2014

Canada Goose

Jobs Hill Rd, Ellington | US-CT

One of my more notable encounters with this species was when I chased barnacle-goose and by proxy pink-footed-goose and greater-white-fronted-goose . These guys are abundant, and I’m always glad to see them. Another great sighting was at Farmington Meadows when hundreds began flying in from the distance which created these faint black lines in the sky.
